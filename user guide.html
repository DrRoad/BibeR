Introduction

To provide an easier way to summarise your customed scientific literature library, I developed this tool and shared with my colleagues. Now, I decide to share with you, presenting a thought of what I and R language can contribute to bibliometric analysis which is "widely" used by librarians and small group of scientists.

How to use it?

Note: a subscribed access to Web of Science is required.

Please follow the steps:

First of all, make sure you select the "Web of Science Core collection", otherwise inadequate information would be extracted.

Step 1: select the "Web of Science Core collection" database

You'd better search certain keywords that could represent a certain research field (this is on your own, not included in this tutorial) in Web of Science Core collection database. Then save the records on your computer.

Step 2: search and save .txt file in a right way.

You could drag and resize your browser (at least IE9+ and other popular browsers) to take a look at these charts, because I set up the width 800 pixels. Hope you enjoy them!
